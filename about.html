The life of Hedy

Hedy Lamarr (/ˈhɛdi/; born Hedwig Eva

Maria Kiesler; November 9, 1914[a] – January 19, 2000) was

an Austrian-born American actress and inventor. After a brief
early film career in Czechoslovakia, including the controversial

erotic romantic drama Ecstasy (1933), she fled from her first husband,

Friedrich Mandl, and secretly moved to Paris. Traveling to London, she

met Louis B. Mayer, who offered her a film contract in Hollywood.Lamarr
became a film star with her performance in the romantic drama Algiers (1938).
[2] She achieved further success with the Western Boom Town (1940) and the drama
White Cargo (1942). Lamarr's most successful film was the religious epic Samson
and Delilah (1949).[3] She also acted on television before the release of her final
film in 1958. She was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1960.
